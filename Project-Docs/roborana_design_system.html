Why two palettes: a regulated flow needs universally-understood caution (amber) and danger (red) signals that green/blue can't carry. So: brand colors for identity, a small functional set for state. This is intentional, not drift.
Hard brand rule: never green text on a white background. Green appears only on dark surfaces, or as a filled badge/button. The Available badge uses Deep green as a fill with dark text — compliant.

GDo & Don't

DO

  • Use Focus-blue headers with the white logo + green accent.
  • Use Kanit everywhere; Bold for titles & subheads.
  • Use filled status badges with dark/white text.
  • Reserve red strictly for irreversible/blocked states.
  • Use SVG or text glyphs for icons.

DON'T

  • Never put green text on a white background.
  • No emojis anywhere — platform-wide rule.
  • Don't invent new brand colors; status colors are the only functional additions.
  • Don't mix other fonts in with Kanit.
  • Don't use red or amber as decoration — they mean something.
